Booba

Booba is one of the most influential figures in French rap. He's known for his gritty lyrics and hardcore style, with hit songs like "DKR" and "Nero Nemesis." Booba has a massive following in France and beyond.

Damso

Damso is a versatile Belgian-Congolese rapper known for his introspective lyrics, successful albums like "Ipséité" and "Lithopédion," and collaborations with other notable artists. His music explores personal and societal themes, and he has a devoted following in the French-speaking rap scene.

Nekfeu

Nekfeu is a multifaceted artist who's both a rapper and actor. He gained fame as part of the French rap group 1995 and has since released successful solo projects. His songs like "On verra" and "Ma dope" have garnered widespread acclaim.

PNL

PNL is a rap duo consisting of two brothers, Ademo and N.O.S. They are celebrated for their unique, atmospheric sound and introspective lyrics. Their album "Deux Frères" received critical acclaim, and they've become known for tracks like "Au DD" and "DA."

JUL

Jul is a highly prolific rapper and songwriter who has released numerous albums and mixtapes. He's known for his melodic style and a string of chart-topping hits in France, including "La tête dans les nuages" and "Je ne me vois pas briller."

Freeze Corleone

Freeze Corleone is a controversial French rapper known for his unconventional and provocative lyrics. He gained recognition through his online presence and has released albums like "Project Blue Beam" and "L.M.S." His style blends elements of trap and drill, and his content often includes references to conspiracy theories and controversial themes, which have sparked debates and controversies within the French rap scene.
